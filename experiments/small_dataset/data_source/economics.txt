Economics is the study of how individuals, businesses, and governments allocate resources
to meet needs and wants. It examines production, distribution, and consumption of goods
and services. Economists analyze patterns and trends to understand how markets function
and how policies affect economic growth.  Two main branches of economics are
microeconomics and macroeconomics. Microeconomics focuses on individual consumers and
businesses, exploring topics such as pricing, supply, and demand. Macroeconomics looks at
the broader economy, including inflation, unemployment, and national income. Governments
use economic data to make decisions about taxation, spending, and regulation.
Globalization has connected economies worldwide, creating opportunities and challenges.
Trade agreements allow countries to exchange goods and services, while financial markets
enable investment across borders. However, economic inequality and financial instability
remain concerns.  Understanding basic economic principles helps individuals make informed
decisions about spending, saving, and investing. As economies evolve with technology and
innovation, economic analysis becomes increasingly important. Studying economics provides
insights into how societies manage resources and pursue sustainable growth.

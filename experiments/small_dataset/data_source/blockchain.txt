Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger technology that records transactions securely
across multiple computers. Each transaction is stored in a block, and blocks are linked
together in a chain, creating a transparent and tamper-resistant record. Initially
developed for cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, blockchain has expanded into many other
applications.  One key advantage of blockchain is its transparency. Transactions are
visible to participants, reducing the need for intermediaries. This feature makes
blockchain useful in finance, supply chain management, and digital identity verification.
Smart contracts, which execute automatically when conditions are met, further enhance
blockchain’s functionality.  Businesses are exploring blockchain to improve efficiency and
trust in operations. For example, supply chains can use blockchain to track products from
origin to delivery, ensuring authenticity. Governments are also considering blockchain for
secure voting systems and record management.  Despite its potential, blockchain faces
challenges such as scalability, energy consumption, and regulatory uncertainty. Ongoing
research aims to develop more efficient and sustainable solutions. As technology matures,
blockchain is likely to play a significant role in building transparent and secure digital
ecosystems across industries.

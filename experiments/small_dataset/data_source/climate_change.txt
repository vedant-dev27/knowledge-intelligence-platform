Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns,
largely driven by human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation. These
activities release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane into the atmosphere,
trapping heat and causing the planet’s average temperature to rise. Over time, this
warming leads to melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and more frequent extreme weather
events.  The impact of climate change is visible worldwide. Coastal regions face flooding
risks, while agricultural areas experience unpredictable rainfall and droughts.
Biodiversity is also threatened as many species struggle to adapt to rapidly changing
environments. Governments and organizations are working to reduce emissions through
renewable energy adoption, energy efficiency, and sustainable practices.  Individual
actions also contribute to climate solutions. Reducing energy consumption, using public
transportation, and supporting eco-friendly products can collectively make a difference.
Technological innovations such as carbon capture and green hydrogen offer hope for
mitigating environmental damage.  Addressing climate change requires global cooperation
and long-term planning. While the challenge is significant, increased awareness and
scientific progress are creating opportunities to build a more sustainable future.
Protecting the environment today will determine the quality of life for future
generations.

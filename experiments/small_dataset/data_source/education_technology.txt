Education technology, often called EdTech, refers to the use of digital tools and
platforms to enhance teaching and learning. Online learning platforms, virtual classrooms,
and interactive applications have transformed traditional education methods. Students can
now access educational content from anywhere, making learning more flexible and
personalized.  Digital tools such as learning management systems help teachers organize
lessons, track progress, and communicate with students. Multimedia resources, including
videos and simulations, make complex concepts easier to understand. Artificial
intelligence is also being used to create adaptive learning systems that tailor content to
individual student needs.  EdTech gained significant attention during global disruptions
that forced schools to adopt remote learning. While challenges such as internet access and
digital literacy remain, technology continues to expand educational opportunities. Hybrid
learning models combining online and in-person instruction are becoming increasingly
common.  The future of education technology lies in making learning more engaging,
accessible, and efficient. As innovation continues, educators must balance technological
integration with human interaction to ensure meaningful learning experiences. Proper
implementation of EdTech can empower students and teachers alike, shaping the future of
education worldwide.
